# Pixar Production FP&A Budget & Analytics Pipeline

## 📌 Project Overview
This repository contains an end-to-end Corporate Financial Planning & Analysis (FP&A) data pipeline modeled around Pixar Animation Studios' production ecosystem. The project simulates real-world corporate financial challenges: handling chaotic, unformatted raw data across concurrent multi-movie pipelines, cleaning and standardizing the data programmatically via SQL, visualizing variances with an interactive executive dashboard, and translating those insights into strategic cost-mitigation business solutions.

---

## 🛠️ Tech Stack & Tools
* **Data Generation:** Python (Vanilla scripting for dirty data simulation)
* **Data Engineering & ETL:** MySQL / MySQL Workbench
* **Business Intelligence:** Tableau Desktop
* **Strategic Delivery:** Corporate Finance Frameworks & Executive Memorandums

## 📑 Executive Brief & Variance Analysis

> ### MEMORANDUM
>
> **TO:** Pixar Executive Leadership (CFO / VP of Production)  
> **FROM:** Lead FP&A Analyst  
> **SUBJECT:** Q1 2026 Business Unit Budget Variance Analysis & Cost Optimization Strategy  
>
> ---
>
> ### I. Executive Summary
> Total operational and production expenditures for Q1 2026 closed at **278.3M** against a budgeted baseline of **259.8M**. This represents a net unfavorable budget variance of **+[+$267.5M] and Burn Rate Percentage 7.1%**. While core legacy franchise assets remain stable, targeted capital leakages within cloud-compute workloads and creative workflow bottlenecks require immediate intervention to secure pipeline margins ahead of Q2 milestones.
>
> ### II. Key Variance Deep-Dive & Root-Cause Analysis
>
> * **Technical Operations (Render Farm): Unfavorable Variance**
>   * *Root Cause:* Filter analysis of **Project Elio** reveals severe compute overages in February and March. This was driven by unprecedented algorithmic demands for high-fidelity fluid dynamics and environmental geometry, forcing heavy reliance on premium peak-hour cloud infrastructure.
>   * *Financial Impact:* Variable technical costs directly compressed the overall animation pipeline margin by 240 basis points.
>
> * **Pre-Production & Story: Favorable Variance**
>   * *Root Cause:* **Project Hoppers** concluded the quarter under budget due to directorial creative approval delays, freezing voice talent casting and internal labor drawdowns.
>   * *Strategic Risk:* This is a **false-favorable variance**. Deferring this spending creates a downstream bottleneck, compressing the Q2/Q3 animation timeline and insuring premium-rate overtime expenses next quarter to meet the theatrical release window.
>
> ### III. Strategic Cost Optimization Recommendations
>
> 1. **Implement Algorithmic Compute Shifting:** Transition 35% of non-critical, non-peak render cycles to automated public cloud "spot-instances" rather than running full-scale on-premise peak capacity. Establish a mandatory efficiency threshold before executing heavy renders.
> 2. **Dynamic Capital & Headcount Reallocation:** Reallocate the surplus capital generated by the *Project Hoppers* pre-production freeze to scale up mid-tier contract animation teams in Q2 to avoid expensive crunch-time premiums later in the fiscal year.
> 3. **Configure Rolling Variance Alerts:** Establish real-time data threshold filters within the Tableau pipeline to automatically flag any line-item expense category exceeding a ±10% variance over a rolling 14-day window.
